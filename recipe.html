Caffe Americano

Ingredients:

  1. Deselect All
  2. 1 shot espresso coffee
  3. Boiling water
  4. Steamed milk
  5. Raw sugar cube

Description

Make a shot of espresso coffee and pour it into a 6-ounce cup. Add boiling water into the cup until the coffee reaches the top. Have steamed milk on the side to add, along with a sugar cube.

Cortado

Ingredients:

  1. 4 ounce Gibraltar glass
  2. 2 shots of espresso
  3. 2-3 ounces steamed milk
  4. 16.5 grams of ground espresso

Description

Gather about 16.5 ounces of espresso coffee. Grind. Pour 2-3 ounces of milk into milk pitcher. Extract two shots of espresso into Gibraltar glass. While espresso is extracting, steam your milk. Make sure there isn't any foam. Milk should be only a little hot to the touch. Slowly pour milk in espresso. Enjoy!

Mochaccino

Ingredients:

  1. 1/3 cup of milk or any milk substitute
  2. 1-2 espresso shots brewed with Keurig of your favorite ground coffee
  3. White chocolate sauce
  4. Whipped cream (optional)

Description

Brew your espresso shots with Keurig. Mix your coffee shot with chocolate powder or chocolate sauce. Fill a cup with milk and froth using your Keurig or with a milk frother. Pour the steamed milk over your coffee shots. Finish off your drink by scooping the milk foam on top of your beverage. Finish your drink with whipped cream (Optional).

Espresso Macchiato

Ingredients:

  1. 18g ground espresso
  2. or 1 espresso pod
  3. 50-100ml milk
  4. Very little water

Description

Heat the milk on a small saucepan over medium to high heat. Continue to heat for 2-3 minutes until a lot of steam is coming off of the milk. Don't let the milk boil or scald. Remove the milk from the stove and use an electric hand frother to froth the milk. The milk will separate into two layers: a layer of steamed milk on the bottom and a milk foam layer on top. Use a spoon to scoop off just the milk foam and place it on top of the espresso. Serve immediately. Enjoy!

Irish Coffee

Ingredients:

  1. 2 teaspoons packed brown sugar, to taste
  2. 4 ounces strong, hot coffee
  3. 1 1/2 ounces Irish whiskey
  4. 1 ounce heavy cream, lightly whipped

Description

Gather the ingredients.Place the brown sugar into a warm Irish coffee glass, mug, or other heatproof glass.Add the coffee and Irish whiskey.Stir until the sugar is dissolved.Float the lightly whipped heavy cream on top by slowly pouring it over the back of a spoon.Do not stir. Instead, drink the Irish coffee through the cream. Enjoy.

Cafe au Lait

Ingredients:

  1. 1 part hot strong coffee, French roast
  2. 1 part milk, steamed

Description

Gather the ingredients.Into a large cup, pour equal parts of strong hot coffee and steamed milk, reserving the foam.Stir.Spoon reserved foam on top and serve hot.If you're artistic, you might want to try your hand at using the steamed milk to create latte art, or simply give it a sprinkle of cinnamon.Enjoy.

Cold Brew

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup coarsely ground coffee
  2. 4 cups filtered or distilled water

Description

Gather the ingredients.Grind 1 cup of coarsely ground coffee beans.In a large jar, add the ground coffee. Slowly pour 4 cups of water over the beans as you stir until the coffee is thoroughly saturated.Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap. Place it in the refrigerator and let it steep for at least 12 hours. You can brew up to 24 hours, depending on your personal taste.Filter the beans from the coffee. Pour slowly and stop as needed to avoid overflowing the filter.Store filtered cold-brew in an air-tight container in the refrigerator. Instant Coffee

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup milk (or water)
  2. 1 ounce hot cocoa mix (approximately 2 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon)
  3. 1 1/2 to 2 teaspoons instant coffee
  4. 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Description

Gather the ingredients.Bring the milk (or water) to a simmer.Place the cocoa mix and instant coffee granules in amounts for one serving (as directed by each) into an empty mug, along with the cinnamon.Stir in a small amount of the heated milk to create a paste with the coffee mixture. This is easier to do with a fork than it is with a spoon.Gradually add the rest of the hot liquid, while stirring. Serve hot.

Cappuchino

Ingredients:

  1. 2 tablespoons finely ground dark roast coffee
  2. 4 ounces water
  3. 4 ounces milk

Description

Gather the ingredients.Place the water into the boiler of your espresso machine.Place the 2 tablespoons (2 shots) of ground coffee into the portafilter.Place the portafilter into your espresso machine's group head and lock it in place by turning it to the right.Place a demitasse cup or the glass carafe that came with your espresso machine under the group head and pull the shot for 23 to 30 seconds, or until 2 ounces of espresso is yielded. Typically, there is a lever, switch, or button to start this process.Once the shot is pulled, foam the milk.Make sure to keep the tip of the wand toward the side of the container. This will create a vortex with the milk.Once the milk has foamed to double its size, turn the steam wand off.Top the espresso with foamed milk right after foaming. When initially poured, cappuccino is only espresso and foam, but the liquid milk quickly settles out of the foam to create the (roughly) equal parts foam, steamed milk, and espresso for which cappuccino is known.
